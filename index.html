About Me

I am a life science researcher interested in understanding how mechanical cues regulate cell-state transitions during cutaneous wound healing. My work focuses on epithelial mechanotransduction, cytoskeletal dynamics, and epigenetic regulation.

My research explores how actin-dependent mechanical signaling influences the nuclear localization and function of epigenetic regulators such as DNMT3A, and how these mechanisms contribute to tissue repair and regeneration.

Research Interests

  • Cutaneous wound healing
  • Mechanobiology and mechanotransduction
  • Actin cytoskeleton dynamics
  • Epigenetic regulation (DNMT3A)
  • ERK / MAPK signaling pathways

Research Experience

I have hands-on experience with keratinocyte differentiation and wound-healing models, pharmacological perturbations, confocal microscopy, and quantitative image analysis. My work integrates cell biology, signaling, and imaging approaches to understand epithelial tissue repair.
